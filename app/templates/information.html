Course Information

Computer Science is one of the most difficult course in Oxbridge in terms of its application rate (# candidates/# offers). It is also one of the most popular courses in other British universities.

This course has many different variants which are joint majors between computer science and other courses (include but not limit to: Mathematics, Business, etc.) or are more field-specific (e.g. Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, etc.).

Over the past four years, I have accumulated a great range of experiences about CS Oxbridge application from my own experience, feedbacks from previous candidates, information online and information from my tutors in Oxford. All those experiences are concentrated in this course.

This course consists of four main parts: Mathematics. Logics, Computer Science and some other content. Mathematics is the most important part, because interviewers are taking into consideration that some candidates have never learnt programming before or taken Computer Science subject at A-level. However those candidates may have very strong mathematical and logical thinking abilities, especially those fields which are related to Computer Science.

Mathematics
  • Functions
  • Limits
  • Graph Sketching
  • Calculus
  • Sequences
  • Proof
  • Number Theory
  • Recurrent Problems
Logics
  • Propositional Logic and Predicate Logic
  • Boolean Expressions and Boolean Algebra
  • Logic Problems
Computer Science
  • Basic Programming Concepts
  • Complexity
  • Asymptotics and Big-O Notation
  • Analysis of Simple Algorithms
  • Divide and Conquer Strategy
  • Sorting Algorithms
  • Dynamic Programming
  • Searching Algorithms
Others
  • Simple Game Theory
